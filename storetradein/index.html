What are the steps that I need to take to complete a trade-in?

There are three main steps that need to be completed in order to go through a trade-in:

1. Check the list below to see if it's an accepted model and what the value of the trade-in would be.

2. Check the phone's IMEI number against the Canadian blacklist (choose the Canada IMEI Blacklist option) and verify that the phone is not blacklisted. If it's an Apple device, verify that there is no iCloud account on the device - these phones also have zero value.

3. If it passes, make a test call to a phone you have nearby to verify it works. This will verify the microphone, speaker, and earpiece function perfectly. After that, open the camera (both front and back, if it has) to see if it works.

4. If everything above passes, perfect! The phone is ready to be traded in. Accept the phone and pay out the customer through your preferred method (in-store credit, gift card, etc.). If a part of the functionality does not pass, the phone is only worth the "Broken" price.

5. Store the phone in a safe and secure place. Pickup will happen from your store every 2-7 days, depending on volume.

Note the following about a phone's condition:

Mint condition: the device has no scratches or scuffs anywhere on the body. All of the functions work perfectly. Looks basically brand new.

Good condition: the device has some light scratches on the body. There are no large scratches or scuffs. No cracks or dents anywhere on the body. All of the functions work perfectly.

Rough condition: the device has a lot of scratches and/or scuffs on the body. There are dents on the body. All of the functions work perfectly. NOTE: the screen is not cracked.

Broken condition: the device has an issue and is not functioning perfectly, such as a cracked screen, missing buttons, or doesn't turn on. The device is still intact and does not have water damage.
